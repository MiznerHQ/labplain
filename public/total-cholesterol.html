What does your
Total Cholesterol result mean?

Heart Health
⚠️ Educational only. LabPlain does not provide medical advice or diagnosis. Always discuss your specific results with your healthcare provider.
What this test measures

The Total Cholesterol test measures all the cholesterol in your blood combined — HDL, LDL, and other types.

Normal reference range
Below 200 mg/dL (desirable); 200–239 mg/dL (borderline high)

Reference ranges can vary slightly between labs. Always compare your result to the range printed on your own lab report.

What your result might indicate
↑ If High

High total cholesterol increases heart disease risk, but the breakdown into HDL and LDL matters more than the total number alone.

↓ If Low

Very low total cholesterol is uncommon and can sometimes be associated with nutritional issues or hyperthyroidism.

What to do next
Never panic over total cholesterol alone — always ask your doctor for the full lipid panel breakdown.
