Prolactin Blood Test: What High or Low Prolactin Really Means

A prolactin blood test measures the level of prolactin hormone in your bloodstream. High prolactin can affect periods, fertility, breast milk production, testosterone, libido, and headaches. Here's what your results may actually mean.

Typical Prolactin Reference Range

Normal prolactin ranges vary by lab, sex, pregnancy status, medications, and time of day. Levels are naturally higher during pregnancy, stress, sleep, and breastfeeding.

Adult Women 4–23 ng/mL
Adult Men 3–15 ng/mL
Pregnancy Much higher

Slightly elevated prolactin is common and does not automatically mean a pituitary tumor.

Common Reasons Doctors Order It

  • Irregular or missing periods
  • Unexpected breast milk production
  • Infertility workups
  • Low testosterone symptoms
  • Erectile dysfunction
  • Headaches or vision changes
  • Suspected pituitary disorders

What Does High Prolactin Mean?

High prolactin is called hyperprolactinemia. Mild elevations are common and sometimes temporary. Larger elevations may point to hormone imbalance, medications, thyroid problems, or pituitary gland conditions.

Common Causes of High Prolactin

  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Stress or poor sleep
  • Pituitary adenoma (prolactinoma)
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Antidepressants or antipsychotic medications
  • Birth control pills
  • Chest wall irritation or injury
  • Kidney disease
Women Missing periods, infertility, breast discharge, low libido
Men Low testosterone, erectile dysfunction, low sex drive
Neurologic Symptoms Headaches or vision changes with very high levels

What Does Low Prolactin Mean?

Low prolactin is usually less concerning than high prolactin and often causes no symptoms at all. In some cases, it may reflect pituitary dysfunction or medication effects.

Possible Causes of Low Prolactin

  • Dopamine-enhancing medications
  • Pituitary gland dysfunction
  • Severe illness or hormonal imbalance
  • Postpartum pituitary injury (rare)

Doctors usually interpret prolactin together with other hormones like TSH, LH, FSH, estrogen, or testosterone.

When Is Prolactin Considered Dangerous?

Mild elevations are often harmless. Doctors become more concerned when prolactin levels are very high, symptoms are severe, or imaging suggests a pituitary mass.

Usually Mild Slightly above normal after stress, exercise, or poor sleep
Moderate Concern Persistent elevation with fertility or hormone symptoms
Needs Evaluation Very high prolactin plus headaches or vision problems

Prolactin levels can temporarily rise from stress before the blood draw itself.

How To Prepare For a Prolactin Blood Test

  • Avoid intense exercise before testing
  • Try to reduce stress before the blood draw
  • Morning testing is often preferred
  • Tell your doctor about all medications
  • Some doctors recommend fasting beforehand

Because prolactin changes throughout the day, your doctor may repeat the test if the result is borderline abnormal.
