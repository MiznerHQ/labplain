What does your
Neutrophils result mean?

Blood Count
⚠️ Educational only. LabPlain does not provide medical advice or diagnosis. Always discuss your specific results with your healthcare provider.
What this test measures

The Neutrophils test measures your most abundant white blood cell — the first responders of your immune system that rush to fight bacterial infections and heal tissue damage.

Normal reference range
1.8–7.7 × 10³/µL  |  40–70% of total white blood cells

Reference ranges can vary slightly between labs. Always compare your result to the range printed on your own lab report.

What your result might indicate
↑ If High

High neutrophils (neutrophilia) usually means your body is fighting a bacterial infection. It can also be triggered by stress, steroid medications, smoking, or intense exercise.

↓ If Low

Low neutrophils (neutropenia) can leave you vulnerable to serious infections. Causes include viral infections, certain medications, autoimmune conditions, or bone marrow problems.

What to do next
Neutrophils are part of your CBC differential — always look at this number alongside your total WBC count. A single abnormal result is often temporary; your doctor will look at trends over time.
