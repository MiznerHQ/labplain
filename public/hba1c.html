What Is HbA1c?

The HbA1c test measures the percentage of your hemoglobin—the protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen—that is coated with sugar (glycated). Because red blood cells live for about 3 months, this test reveals your average blood sugar levels over the past 90 days.

Unlike standard fasting glucose tests which change day by day based on your last meal, stress levels, or sleep, HbA1c provides a stable historical record. Your doctor uses it to screen for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes, or to see how well an existing blood sugar management plan is working.

📊

HbA1c Reference Ranges

Level (%) Category What It Means
Below 5.7% Normal Optimal glucose regulation over the past 3 months.
5.7% – 6.4% Prediabetes Early signs of cellular insulin resistance; lifestyle changes recommended.
6.5% and above Diabetes Diagnostic threshold for clinical diabetes; active management needed.

* Target ranges can vary for pregnant individuals, older adults, and those managing long-term chronic conditions. Always interpret with your physician.

Symptoms of Unbalanced Blood Sugar

Mildly elevated HbA1c (prediabetes) typically causes no symptoms at all. However, as levels move higher into the diabetes range, you may notice distinct systemic signals:

Increased Thirst (Polydipsia) Your kidneys work overtime to filter and absorb the excess sugar, pulling fluid from tissues.
Frequent Urination (Polyuria) As fluids are pulled from your body, you find yourself needing to use the bathroom far more often.
Unexplained Fatigue Because sugar stays in the bloodstream instead of entering cells, your body lacks its main energy source.
Blurred Vision High blood sugar pulls fluid from the lenses of your eyes, changing your ability to focus properly.

Common Causes of High HbA1c

Insulin Resistance Cells stop responding efficiently to insulin, leaving glucose locked out in the blood.
High-Refined Carbohydrate Diet Frequent spikes from processed foods and added sugars overwhelm metabolic pathways.
Physical Inactivity Skeletal muscles are prime glucose sinks; lack of movement reduces natural sugar clearance.
Chronic Stress & Lack of Sleep Elevated cortisol and epinephrine trigger the liver to dump extra glucose into circulation.
📉

Can HbA1c Be Too Low?

An HbA1c below 4.0% is uncommon but can sometimes signal underlying health or blood-related conditions. Because the test relies on red blood cells, anything that speeds up their lifecycle can falsely lower the score:

Hemolytic Anemia Red blood cells die off too quickly, meaning they spend less time accumulating sugar coats.
Chronic Liver or Kidney Disease Can alter how red blood cells are manufactured and sustained within the body.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to fast before an HbA1c test? No. Because it measures a 3-month average, eating or fasting right before the test won't change your result. You can take it at any time of day.
How fast can I bring down my HbA1c score? Because it tracks the 90-day lifecycle of red blood cells, meaningful changes typically show up on bloodwork after 3 months of consistent lifestyle or dietary adjustments.
