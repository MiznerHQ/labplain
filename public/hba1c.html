Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)

The HbA1c test measures the percentage of your hemoglobin—the protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen—that is coated with sugar (glycated). Because red blood cells live for about 3 months, this test reveals your **average blood sugar levels over the past 90 days**.

Official Reference Ranges

Normal
Below 5.7%

Optimal glucose regulation.

Prediabetes
5.7% – 6.4%

Indicates early cellular insulin resistance.

Diabetes
6.5% or Higher

Diagnostic threshold for clinical diabetes.

Why Doctors Order This Test

Unlike standard finger-prick fasting glucose tests which fluctuate wildly based on your last meal, stress levels, or sleep patterns, HbA1c cannot be cheated. It provides an un-wipeable diagnostic record used to identify insulin resistance, screen for type 2 diabetes, or monitor the efficacy of ongoing metabolic treatment plans.
