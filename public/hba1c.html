Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)

Your 3-month blood sugar average. Unlike a daily finger prick, this measures the percentage of sugar locked onto your red blood cells.

Optimal Range
< 5.7%
Prediabetes Range
5.7% - 6.4%
High Risk (Diabetes)
≥ 6.5%
Test Type
Glycated Hemoglobin

What is an HbA1c Test?

When glucose enters your bloodstream, it naturally binds to a protein in your red blood cells called hemoglobin. Once bound, it stays there for the lifespan of that cell (roughly 3 months). An A1c test measures the exact percentage of your hemoglobin cells that are coated in sugar, offering a steady, reliable view of your long-term blood sugar control.

Result PercentageStatus ClassificationClinical Meaning
Under 5.7%Normal / OptimalHealthy metabolic function and stable glucose regulation.
5.7% to 6.4%PrediabetesIndicates insulin resistance. A critical window to adjust diet and lifestyle choices.
6.5% or HigherDiabetes Risk RangeConfirms sustained high blood sugar. Requires medical evaluation and planning.

Primary Drivers of High A1c

  • Diets dominant in refined carbohydrates, sodas, and ultra-processed foods.
  • Insulin resistance, where body tissue rejects circulating insulin.
  • Extended periods of physical inactivity.
  • Sustained high cortisol from chronic psychological stress or poor sleep patterns.

Early Indicator Signs

The Gradual Shift

Prediabetes levels rarely cause sudden physical changes. Symptoms appear slowly as percentages approach or cross the 6.5% line.

Watch for creeping signs like frequent fatigue, unexplained thirst, frequent urination, or blurred vision.
