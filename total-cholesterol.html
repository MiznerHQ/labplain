Total Cholesterol

The total accumulation of cholesterol circulating in your bloodstream, combining both your "good" and "bad" carrier particles.

Optimal Range
< 200 mg/dL
Borderline High
200 - 239
High Risk Value
≥ 240 mg/dL
Test Type
Lipid Panel

What is Total Cholesterol?

Your Total Cholesterol score is a composite number. It is calculated by adding your HDL (high-density lipoprotein), your LDL (low-density lipoprotein), and 20% of your Triglyceride levels together. While it gives a solid baseline reading of your overall lipid health, it is always best interpreted alongside your standalone HDL and LDL metrics.

Result Range Classification What It Means
Under 200 mg/dL Optimal Indicates a lower baseline risk for cardiovascular disease.
200 – 239 mg/dL Borderline High A clear signal to review dietary choices, exercise habits, and lifestyle factors.
240 mg/dL and above High Risk Associated with double the risk of heart disease compared to optimal levels. Requires medical review.

Common Causes of High Levels

  • Diets rich in trans fats, saturated fats, and processed sugars.
  • Sedentary lifestyle or lack of routine cardiovascular exercise.
  • Genetic predispositions (such as Familial Hypercholesterolemia).
  • Underlying metabolic changes, smoking, or chronic stress.

Associated Symptoms

The Silent Number

High total cholesterol typically carries absolutely zero physical symptoms. Most individuals feel perfectly fine until a regular blood draw reveals an elevation.

In extremely rare, severe genetic cases, physical deposits of fat called xanthomas might form around tendons or skin surfaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to fast before this test?
Traditionally, a 9 to 12-hour fast is requested before a full lipid panel to ensure triglycerides don't temporarily skew the final calculation. However, modern medical guidelines frequently allow non-fasting tests. Check with your doctor's specific lab instructions.
Can a high total cholesterol score be safe?
Occasionally, yes. If an individual has exceptionally high levels of protective HDL ("good") cholesterol, it can artificially inflate the overall total score despite a perfectly healthy, low LDL profile. Always assess the specific ratios.
